pollution

The word pollution was derived from the Latin word pollution, which means to make dirty. Pollution is the process of making the environment pollute the water and the air by adding harmful substances. Pollution can take the form of any substance (solid, liquid, or gas) or energy (such as radioactivity, heat, sound, or light). Pollutants, the components of pollution, can be either foreign substances/energies or naturally occurring contaminants.Pollution causes an imbalance in the environment. Air Pullution :-

Air pollution mostly occurs in industrial towns in India and abroad. While technological developments and industrialization have led to creation of wealth and prosperity, it has also caused a massive pollution of the environment.The harmful burning of fuels such as coal and petroleum in some major industries like automobile, electrical power plants, etc., cause air pollution. Smoke emitted from factories and houses pollute the atmosphere. Excessive smoke and fumes have a very bad effect on plants, animals and human beings.

Water Pullution :-

Water Pollution refers to the presence of impurities and harmful elements in the water. It is a source of many deadly diseases that cause harm, diseases, and even kill human civilization. The polluted water is not fit for drinking and is harmful to living beings such as animals, birds, and human beings.

Land Pullution :-

Land pollution refers to the deterioration of the earth’s land surfaces, at and below ground level. The cause is the accumulation of solid and liquid waste materials that contaminate groundwater and soil. These waste materials are often referred to as municipal solid waste (MSW), which includes both hazardous and non-hazardous waste. When waste is deposited onto an area of land, the permeability of the soil formations below the waste can increase or reduce the risk of land pollution. The higher the permeability of the soil, the more likely that land pollution will occur. That’s why the Texas Disposal Systems Landfill, located just outside of Austin, Texas, was built in an ideal area. By utilizing the natural shale and clay in the land, the risk of land pollution has been significantly decreased.

Pollution is the introduction of contaminants into the natural environment that cause adverse change. Although environmental pollution can be caused by natural events, the word pollution generally implies that the contaminants have an anthropogenic source – that is, a source created by human activities. Pollution is often classed as point source or nonpoint source pollution. In 2015, pollution killed 9 million people worldwide.
